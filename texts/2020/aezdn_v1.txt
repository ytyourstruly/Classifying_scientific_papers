First approach to the semantic analysis of the academic
production of the Faculty of Sociology of the Santo Tomás
University (Colombia) teachers
Miguel Urra Canales
Decano de la Facultad de Sociología
Grupo de Investigación “Estudios Interdisciplinarios de la Sociedad y la Cultura”
Abstract:
This article presents the results of a bibliometric investigation, whose object of study is the academic
production of the faculty of the Faculty of Sociology of the Santo Tomás University. Through the
elaboration of word clouds with the titles of the publications of the teachers, it is concluded that
there is a great semantic variety in the analysis of all the titles produced, a greater specificity if only
the last five are taken into account years and interdisciplinary work that enriches the collection of
analysis categories deployed in research projects.
Key words: academic production, semantics, bibliometrics, sociology
1.- Approach
This research follows in the wake of those carried out in 2018 and 2019, on intellectual
trajectories and fields of action, whose object of study is the generation of knowledge by
the teachers of the Faculty of Sociology of the Santo Tomás University (Colombia ). On this
occasion, a semantic approach has been made to the academic production of this teaching
team, from the titles of the research they have published.
In previous years, it was concluded that the Faculty has a “team of young researchers and
researchers, which addresses 124 fields of study and in which qualitative methodology
prevails” (Urra, M. 2018c) and that it has an “adequate alignment of research products with
the Society field of action and a still timid alignment with the Environment field of action
(Urra, M. 2019b). On this occasion, we start from three main hypotheses:
1) If you take into account all the titles produced by the teachers, you will find a great
variety.
2) If only the last five years are taken as a reference, which is the time that the majority of
teachers have been working in the Faculty, a greater specificity will be found.
3) There must be some difference between the products published by educational
sociologists and training sociologists and the rest.
2.- Methodology:
To address the hypotheses raised, the production reported in GoogleScholar by the 17
teachers of the Faculty was taken into account. In total, there are 113 documents.
As general criteria, titles were translated into Spanish if they were in other languages, and
the first teacher who appeared in the final publication was assigned as the main author of
collective works.
With the titles, a word cloud construction process was carried out, with the
clouddepalabras.es application, eliminating the connectors and prepositions and taking into
account the words that appeared 3 or more times.
3.-Results
First, we are going to analyze the word cloud resulting from taking as a reference the titles
of all the research products, of all the authors and of all the years.
As can be seen, Colombia (for example, Aliaga, F. et. Al. 2017, 2018, 2019 and 2020; Alzate,
J, F, and Gualteros, MI 2012; Gamba, A. 2017; Idrobo, J. 2020 and Narváez, G. 2016), Bogotá
(for example Acosta E, et.al. 2016; Aliaga, F. et.al. 2020; Bravo, CA and Aliaga, F. 2018;
Idrobo, J. 2018; Jaime. E. et. al. 2020; Pinzón, JA 2012; Sáenz, H. 2010 and 2011; Uribe, C.
2016 and Urra, M. et al. 2019a), the social (lo social) (for example, Acosta, C. 2019; Gamba
2011; Jaime. E. et. Al. 2020; Leiva, A. 2020; Urra, M. 2010 and Urra, M. 2018b) and peace
(paz) (for example, Acosta, C. et. Al. 2019; Gamba, A. 2006; Idrobo , J. 2020a and 2020c;
Narváez, G. 2016 and 2020) appear with a greater presence.
They are followed by territories (territorios), neighborhoods (barrios), integration
(integración), Cauca and relationships (relaciones).
With a lesser presence, Ecuador, Latin America, immigrants (inmigrantes), imaginary
(imaginarios), peoples (los pueblos), conflicto (conflicto), politics (política), environmental
(ambiente) and urban (urbano) stand out. It is also interesting to observe the period 2015-
2018, which shows the work carried out in these years.
Second, the word cloud resulting from the titles published only in the last five years 2016-
2020 appears.
As can be seen, the semantic network that has more than three occurrences is much
smaller. Colombia, Bogotá, the social (social), peace (paz) and case studies (estudio de caso)
also stand out. They are followed by the imaginaries (imaginarios), Latin America, Ecuador,
the reflections (relfexiones), the relationships (relaciones), the return (retorno) and the
triplet that form the themes of leasing (arrendamiento), construction (construcción) and
residential (residencial).
Third, two word clouds have been made, separating teachers into two large groups,
according to whether they have sociological training or not. In the Faculty, a team of
graduates works in sociology, but also in history, anthropology, political science, economics,
law and philosophy.

As can be seen, while always dealing with social issues, different categories of analysis are
identified. For example, the group of sociologists work imaginary (imaginarios) (for
example, Aliaga, F. 2008a, 2008b, 2012, 2019; Aliaga, F. and Carretero, E. 2016), community
(comunidad) (for example, Acosta, C. et al. 2019 and Urra, M. 2018b) inclusion (Aliaga, F.
2020) or integration (integración) (Aliaga, F. 2008b, 2012 and 2020). The group of other
professionals works on categories such as resistance (resistencias) (Idrobo, J. 2013, 2018
and 2020a) rights (derechos) (Añaños, K., and Hernández, B. 2019) or relationships
(relaciones) (Sáenz, H. 2010 and 2016).
4.-Conclusions
The results obtained allow us to answer the hypotheses raised.
As for the great semantic variety in the analysis of all the titles produced by the teachers, it
is confirmed to a great extent. 18 main words are identified, among which Colombia,
Bogotá, social and peace stand out, followed by territories, neighborhoods, integration,
Cauca and relationships.
Regarding a greater specificity if you take into account only the last five years, it is also
confirmed to a large extent. Eleven main words are identified, among which Colombia,
Bogotá, social, peace and case studies stand out.
The explanation of these first two findings may be related to the fact that the teachers had
been working on some topics in their research career and, since their arrival at the Faculty,
they have continued to develop them to a certain extent.
Regarding the differences between the products published by educational sociologists and
training sociologists and the rest, it should be noted that the vast majority of the products
analyzed are collectively authored, which, added to this analysis of word clouds, speaks of
a Very interesting interdisciplinary work at the Faculty, where the different professions
enrich the collection of social categories that are worked on in research projects.
In the future, it would be interesting to continue with this line of research, expanding the
analysis to the product summaries, the full text of the products and even the titles of the
bibliographic references of the products. Without a doubt, it is exciting to take as an object
of study the way in which knowledge is produced in an academic facility.
